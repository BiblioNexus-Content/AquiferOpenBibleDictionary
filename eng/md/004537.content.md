## Pound (id: 7798)

POUND
=====

1. The King James Version term for “*mina*,” a weight equal to one pound or half a kilogram ([1 Kings 10:17](https://ref.ly/1Kgs10:17); [Ezra 2:69](https://ref.ly/Ezra2:69); [Nehemiah 7:71](https://ref.ly/Neh7:71-Neh7:72)[–](https://ref.ly/Neh7:71-Neh7:72)[72](https://ref.ly/Neh7:71-Neh7:72)).

    *See* Weights and Measures.

2. A Greek coin *(*mina*)* equal to about three month’s wages ([Luke 19:13](https://ref.ly/Luke19:13)).
3. A Roman measure (*litra* in Greek) equal to about 12 ounces, or .3 kilogram (three\-quarters of a pound). It is only mentioned in [John 12:3](https://ref.ly/John12:3) and [19:39](https://ref.ly/John19:39).

    *See* Weights and Measures.
