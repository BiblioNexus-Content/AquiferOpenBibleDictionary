## Nethaneel, Nethanel (id: 7371)

NETHANEEL, NETHANEL
===================

A common Old Testament name spelled Nethaneel in the King James Version.

1. A son of Zuar and the leader of the tribe of Issachar at the start of the wilderness wanderings of Israel ([Numbers 1:8](https://ref.ly/Num1:8); [2:5](https://ref.ly/Num2:5); [10:15](https://ref.ly/Num10:15)). He represented his relatives at the dedication of the altar ([7:18, 23](https://ref.ly/Num7:18,Num7:23)).
2. A Judahite who was the fourth son of Jesse and brother of King David ([1 Chronicles 2:14](https://ref.ly/1Chr2:14)).
3. One of the priests assigned to blow a trumpet in front of the ark of God when King David moved it to Jerusalem ([1 Chronicles 15:24](https://ref.ly/1Chr15:24)).
4. A Levite and the father of Shemaiah. He was the scribe who recorded the 24 divisions of priests founded when David was king ([1 Chronicles 24:6](https://ref.ly/1Chr24:6)).
5. A Korahite from the tribe of Levi. He was the fifth son of Obed\-edom when David was king ([1 Chronicles 26:4](https://ref.ly/1Chr26:4)).
6. One of the princes sent by King Jehoshaphat to teach the law in the cities of Judah ([2 Chronicles 17:7](https://ref.ly/2Chr17:7)).
7. One of the Levitical officers who was generous in donating animals for sacrifices. He gave these to the Levites for the celebration of the Passover feast when Josiah was king ([2 Chronicles 35:9](https://ref.ly/2Chr35:9)).
8. A priest and one of the six sons of Pashhur. Ezra encouraged Nethanel to divorce his foreign wife after the Israelite return from exile ([Ezra 10:22](https://ref.ly/Ezra10:22)).
9. The leader of the family of the priest Jedaiah. This was when Joiakim was the high priest in Jerusalem after the Israelite return from exile ([Nehemiah 12:21](https://ref.ly/Neh12:21)).
10. One of the priests serving as a musician. He performed at the dedication of the Jerusalem wall during the time of Nehemiah ([Nehemiah 12:36](https://ref.ly/Neh12:36)).
